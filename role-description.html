Data engineer

Find out more about what a data engineer in the Civil Service is expected to do.

Published 25 July 2019
From:
Government Digital Service

Contents

  1. 1. What does a data engineer do?
  2. 2. Role levels
  3. 3. What skills do you need?

A data engineer industrialises the delivery of data products and services into systems and business processes.

This role is part of the Digital, Data and Technology Capability Framework.

1. What does a data engineer do?

As a data engineer, you will:

  • design, build, test and maintain data management systems, making sure they meet business requirements and user needs
  • deliver services that are automated, reliable and secure
  • work closely with data architects and data scientists
  • recommend ways to improve data efficiency and reliability

2. Role levels

There are 4 levels in this role, ranging from data engineer (entry level) to head of data engineering (most senior role). You will find information on the skills you need for these roles in section 3.

  • Download role descriptions for these job levels

2.1 Data engineer

A data engineer delivers the designs set by more senior members of the data engineering community. As a data engineer, you will:

  • implement data flows to connect operational systems, data for analytics and BI systems
  • document source-to-target mappings
  • re-engineer manual data flows to enable scaling and repeatable use
  • support the build of data streaming systems
  • write ETL scripts and code to make sure the ETL process performs optimally
  • develop business intelligence reports that can be re-used
  • build accessible data for analysis

2.2 Senior data engineer

A senior data engineer designs and leads the implementation of data flows to connect operational systems, data for analytics and BI systems. In this role, you will:

  • recognise opportunities to re-use existing data flows
  • lead the build of data streaming systems
  • optimise the code to ensure processes perform optimally
  • lead work on database management

2.3 Lead data engineer

A lead data engineer is responsible for the design and implementation of numerous complex data flows to connect operational systems, data for analytics and BI systems. In this role, you will:

  • recognise and share opportunities to re-use existing data flows between their teams
  • are responsible for the build of data streaming systems
  • coordinate teams and set best practice and standards

2.4 Head of data engineering

A head of data engineering leads multi-functional delivery teams to deliver robust data services for their department, other government departments and private sector partners. In this role, you will:

  • inspire best practice for data products and services within their teams
  • build the data engineering capability by providing technical leadership and career development for the community
  • work with other senior team members to identify, plan, develop and deliver data services

3. What skills do you need?

For this job, you should be able to demonstrate that you have the skills outlined below. All of the skills are required for all roles, but the skill level will be different from role to role.

3.1 Communicating between the technical and the non-technical

You should be able to:

  • communicate effectively across organisational, technical and political boundaries, understanding the context
  • make complex and technical information and language simple and accessible for non-technical audiences
  • advocate and communicate what a team does to create trust and authenticity, and respond to challenge

You also need level-specific skills:

  • Data engineer: you will be able to: translate technical concepts into non-technical language; discuss what communication is required to internal and external stakeholders.
  • Senior data engineer: you will be able to: effectively translate and accurately communicate across technical and non-technical stakeholders; facilitate discussions within a multidisciplinary team, with potentially difficult dynamics; advocate for the team externally; manage differing perspectives.
  • Lead data engineer: you will be able to: listen to the needs of technical and business stakeholders and interpret between them; manage stakeholdersâ€™ expectations and be flexible; communicate both proactively and reactively; facilitate difficult discussions within the team or with diverse senior stakeholders.
  • Head of data engineering: you will be able to: communicate effectively across organisational, technical and political boundaries, understanding the context; make complex and technical information and language simple and accessible for non-technical audiences; be able to advocate and communicate what a team does to create trust and authenticity; respond to challenge.

3.2 Data analysis and synthesis

You should be able to:

  • translate data into valuable insights that inform decisions.
  • involve teams in analytics and synthesis to increase consensus and challenge assumptions
  • identify and utilise the most appropriate analytical techniques
  • have an understanding of analytical tools and be numerate
  • be aware of and keep up to date with advances in digital analytics tools and data manipulation products
  • collect, collate, cleanse, synthesise and interpret data to derive meaningful and actionable insights.

You also need level-specific skills:

  • Data engineer / Senior data engineer: you will undertake data profiling and source system analysis and present clear insights to colleagues to support the end use of the data.
  • Lead data engineer / Head of data engineering: you will: understand and help teams apply a range of techniques for data profiling; source system analysis from a complex single source; bring multiple data sources together in a conformed model for analysis.

3.3 Data development process

You should be able to:

  • integrate and separate data feeds in order to map, produce, transform and test new data products

You also need level-specific skills:

  • Data engineer / Senior data engineer: you will need to: design, build and test data products based on feeds from multiple systems using a range of different storage technologies and/or access methods; create repeatable and reusable products.
  • Lead data engineer: you will need to: design, build and test data products that are complex or large-scale; build teams to complete data integration services.
  • Head of data engineering: you will need to: establish enterprise-scale data integration procedures across the data development lifecycle and ensure that teams adhere to these; manage resources to ensure that data services work effectively at an enterprise level.

3.4 Data integration design

You should be able to:

  • develop fit-for-purpose, resilient, scalable and future-proof data services to meet user needs
  • have a demonstrable understanding of how to expose data from systems (for example, through APIs), link data from multiple systems and deliver streaming services

You also need level-specific skills:

  • Data engineer: you will need to: develop fit-for-purpose, resilient, scalable and future-proof data services to meet user needs; know how to expose data from systems (for example, through APIs), link data from multiple systems and deliver streaming services.
  • Senior data engineer: you will need to: deliver data solutions in accordance with agreed organisational standards that ensure services are resilient, scalable and future-proof.
  • Lead data engineer / Head of data engineering: you will need to: establish standards; keep abreast of best practice in industry and across government; keep those standards up to date and ensure adherence to them.

3.5 Data modelling

You should be able to:

  • produce data models and understand where to use different types of data models
  • understand different tools and be able to compare between different data models
  • reverse engineer a data model from a live system
  • understand industry-recognised data modelling patterns and standards

You also need level-specific skills:

  • Data engineer: you will be able to: communicate effectively across organisational, technical and political boundaries, understanding the context; make complex and technical information and language simple and accessible for non-technical audiences; advocate and communicate what a team does to create trust and authenticity, and can respond to challenge.
  • Senior data engineer: you will be able to: explain the concepts and principles of data modelling; produce, maintain and update relevant data models for specific business needs; reverse-engineer data models from a live system.
  • Lead data engineer: you will be able to: explain the concepts and principles of data modelling; produce relevant data models across multiple subject areas; reverse-engineer data models from a live system; understand industry-recognised data modelling patterns and standards and when to apply them; compare and align different data models.
  • Head of data engineering: you will be able to: understand the concepts and principles of data modelling; produce relevant data models; work across government and into industry, understanding opportunities for re-use and alignment between the data models in different organisations; design how data models should be categorised within the organisation.

3.6 Programming and build (data engineering)

You should be able to:

  • design, write and iterate code from prototype to production-ready
  • understand security, accessibility and version control
  • use a range of coding tools and languages

You also need level-specific skills:

  • Data engineer: you will be able to: design, code, test, correct and document simple programs or scripts under the direction of others.
  • Senior data engineer / Lead data engineer: you will be able to: use and apply agreed standards and tools to design, code, test, correct and document moderate to complex programs and scripts from agreed specifications and subsequent iterations; collaborate with others to review specifications where appropriate.
  • Head of data engineering: you will be able to: set local or team-based standards for programming tools and techniques; select appropriate development methods; advise on the application of standards and methods and ensure compliance; take technical responsibility for all stages and/or iterations in a software development project, providing method-specific technical advice and guidance to project stakeholders.

3.7 Technical understanding (data engineering)

You should be able to:

  • talk about specific technologies that underpin an individualâ€™s ability to deliver the responsibilities and tasks of their role
  • apply the required breadth and depth of technical knowledge

You also need level-specific skills:

  • Data engineer / Senior data engineer: you will be able to: discuss core technical concepts related to your role; apply them with guidance.
  • Lead data engineer: you will be able to: demonstrate a deep understanding of the technical concepts required in your role; explain how these fit into the wider technical landscape.
  • Head of data engineering: you will be able to: discuss and advise on future technology changes that present opportunities for the product or programme.

3.8 Testing

You should be able to:

  • plan, design, manage, execute and report tests, using appropriate tools and techniques
  • work within regulations
  • ensure risks associated with deployment are adequately understood and documented

You also need level-specific skills:

  • Data engineer / Senior data engineer: you will be able to: correctly execute test scripts under supervision; explain the role of testing and how it works.
  • Lead data engineer/Head of data engineering: you will be able to: review requirements, specifications and define test conditions; identify issues and risks associated with work; analyse and report test activities and results.

3.9 Data Innovation

You should be able to:

  • recognise and exploit business opportunities to ensure more efficient and effective performance of organisations
  • explore new ways of conducting business and organisational processes

You also need level-specific skills:

  • Data engineer / Senior data engineer: you will be able to identify opportunities for innovation with new tools and uses of data
  • Lead data engineer: you will be able to: identify areas of innovation in data tools and techniques; recognise appropriate timing for adoption.
  • Head of data engineering: you will be able to: investigate emerging trends in data-related approaches; perform horizon-scanning for the organisation; introduce innovative ways of working.

3.10 Metadata management

You should be able to:

  • discuss a variety of metadata management tools
  • design and maintain the appropriate metadata repositories to enable the organisation to understand their data assets

You also need level-specific skills:

  • Data engineer: you will be able to: work with metadata repositories to complete complex tasks such as data and systems integration impact analysis; maintain a repository to ensure information remains accurate and up to date.
  • Senior data engineer: you will be able to: design an appropriate metadata repository and present changes to existing metadata repositories; discuss a range of tools for storing and working with metadata; provide oversight and advice to more inexperienced members of the team.
  • Lead data engineer: you will be able to: decide on an appropriate metadata repository and present changes to existing metadata repositories; identify a range of tools for storing and working with metadata; provide oversight and advice to more inexperienced members of the team.
  • Head of data engineering: you will be able to: explain how metadata repositories can support different areas of the business; promote and communicate the value of metadata repositories; set up robust governance processes for keeping the repositories up to date.

3.11 Problem resolution (data)

You should be able to:

  • log, analyse and manage problems in order to identify and implement the appropriate solution
  • ensure that the problem is fixed

You also need level-specific skills:

  • Data engineer / Senior data engineer: you need to be able to: discuss the types of problems in databases, data processes, data products and services.
  • Lead data engineer: you need to: ensure that the most appropriate actions are taken to resolve problems as they occur; coordinate teams to resolve problems; implement solutions and preventative measures.
  • Head of data engineering: you need to: anticipate problems and know how to prevent them; explain how the problem fits into the larger picture; articulate the problem and help others to articulate the problem; build problem-solving capabilities in others.
